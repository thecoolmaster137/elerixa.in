Electric Control Panel

APFC Panel (Automatic Power Factor Correction Panel)

An APFC (Automatic Power Factor Correction) panel is an electrical control panel used to monitor and improve the power factor of electrical loads.

The power factor is a measure of how effectively electrical power is utilized by a system. It represents the ratio of real power (kW) to apparent power (kVA) in an AC electrical system.

The APFC panel consists of power factor correction capacitors that are switched in or out based on the load requirements to maintain a desired power factor.

It helps in optimizing power consumption, reducing electricity bills, and improving the overall efficiency of the electrical system.

The APFC panel continuously monitors the power factor and automatically controls the capacitors to maintain a near unity power factor.

MCC (Motor Control Center) and PCC (Power Control Center) Panels

MCC (Motor Control Center) and PCC (Power Control Center) panels are electrical control panels used for motor control and power distribution, respectively

MCC panels are used to control and monitor electric motors in industrial processes. They contain motor starters, contactors, overload relays, and other protective devices for motor control and protection.

PCC panels are used to distribute and control power in large-scale industrial facilities. They receive power from the main power source and distribute it to various loads through circuit breakers, switchgear, and protective devices.

PLC (Programmable Logic Controller), SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition), HMI (Human-Machine Interface) Control Panel

PLC, SCADA, and HMI control panels are used in industrial automation systems to monitor and control processes.

PLC is a digital computer-based control system that uses programmable memory to store instructions and perform control functions. It is used for automating industrial processes, such as assembly lines, machinery control, and manufacturing systems.

SCADA is a system that collects and analyzes real-time data from various sensors and devices. It provides control and monitoring capabilities for large-scale processes or distributed systems.

HMI refers to the graphical user interface through which an operator interacts with the automation system. It allows users to monitor process variables, control devices, and receive alarms or notifications.

Drive Panels

Drive panels, also known as Variable Frequency Drive (VFD) panels or Inverter panels, are used to control the speed and torque of electric motors.

Drive panels consist of VFDs, which convert the incoming AC power into variable frequency and voltage output to control the speed and direction of motors.

These panels find applications in various industries, including HVAC systems, pumps, fans, conveyor belts, and other motor-driven equipment.

They offer energy savings, precise control, and motor protection features such as overload protection, phase loss protection, and over/under voltage protection.

AHF Panel (Active Harmonic Filter Panel)

An AHF (Active Harmonic Filter) panel is used to mitigate harmonics in electrical systems caused by nonlinear loads.

Harmonics are undesirable distortions in the electrical waveform that can cause equipment malfunction, overheating, and power quality issues.

The AHF panel actively monitors the harmonic currents and generates equal and opposite currents to cancel out the harmonics, thus reducing their impact on the system.

It helps in complying with power quality standards, minimizing voltage distortion, and improving the efficiency of the electrical system.
